Overview

Enrolli is a college search and matching platform for high school students and their families. These Terms of Service are written in two parts:

5. College Connection — Affirmative Opt-In Required

This section describes Enrolli's College Connection feature. Participation requires a separate, affirmative opt-in step during account creation. Agreeing to these Terms of Service alone does not enroll you or your student in College Connection.

What College Connection is:

College Connection is the Enrolli feature by which your student's information and your Parent Assessment responses may be shared with admissions offices at colleges your student demonstrates meaningful interest in. The purpose is to enable those colleges to reach out directly to your student with personalized information, financial aid details, scholarship opportunities, and admissions guidance.

How to opt in:

During account creation, you will be presented with a separate College Connection consent step — a clearly labeled checkbox or toggle, distinct from your acceptance of these Terms. You must affirmatively select this option to enroll in College Connection. Your student will separately acknowledge the feature in age-appropriate language during their own account setup. Your parental consent is the legally operative consent for data sharing purposes.

What triggers a College Connection:

A college may receive your student's data when your student demonstrates meaningful interest through one or more of the following behaviors on the Enrolli platform:

  • Spending a significant amount of time viewing that college's profile
  • Returning to that college's profile multiple times
  • Saving that college to their active list or marking it as a favorite

What information is shared with colleges through College Connection:

  • Student name and contact information
  • Student high school name and location
  • Student Matching Quiz results
  • Parent Assessment results
  • Self-reported GPA — not verified by Enrolli or any institution
  • Self-reported test scores — not verified by Enrolli, College Board, ACT, or any testing organization
  • Financial preferences and expectations
  • Behavioral data indicating the student's level of interest in that specific college

What is never shared:

  • Official verified transcripts or school-issued grade records
  • Official test score reports from College Board, ACT, or any testing organization
  • Payment information or financial account details
  • Any information not voluntarily provided to Enrolli by you or your student

Who receives your data:

Only accredited colleges and universities with active undergraduate admissions programs that have entered into a formal College Data Agreement with Enrolli may receive student and parent data through College Connection. We do not share data with unaccredited institutions, for-profit recruiting firms, or any organization unrelated to your student's college search.

Year One data policy:

Enrolli does not share student or parent data with college admissions offices during our first year of operation. We collect and store behavioral and assessment data during year one. Before we activate College Connection for the first time, we will notify you by email and provide an opportunity to review and confirm your opt-in status. The legal infrastructure permitting College Connection is in place from the date these Terms take effect, even if the feature is not yet active.

6. Opting Out of College Connection and the Drop Process

If you opted in to College Connection, you or your student may opt out at any time through your account dashboard. Opting out stops future data sharing. It does not retrieve data already transmitted to colleges prior to your opt-out — once a college has received your student's information through College Connection, that data exists within that college's systems and cannot be recalled by Enrolli.

College Connection page — available to all subscribers:

All Enrolli subscribers — both Tier 1 and Tier 2 — have access to a College Connection page within their account dashboard. This page displays the colleges that have received your student's data through College Connection, the date on which data was transmitted, and the trigger that initiated the connection.

Submitting a drop or opt-out request through Enrolli:

Through the College Connection page, you may submit a drop or opt-out request for any college that has received your student's data. When you submit a request through Enrolli:

  • Enrolli collects each partner college's preferred drop and opt-out questions in advance.
  • You and your student complete a brief form on the College Connection page based on that college's preferred questions.
  • Enrolli submits the completed form to the college's admissions office on your student's behalf.
  • The college is contractually required under their College Data Agreement with Enrolli to process the drop request.
  • You will receive a confirmation message acknowledging that the request has been submitted.

Colleges may follow up with your student once after receiving a drop request — this is standard admissions practice and may include questions about their decision. After that single contact, no further outreach based on Enrolli data is permitted under the college's agreement with Enrolli.

What the drop process does and does not guarantee:

Enrolli's role in the drop process is to transmit your request to the college and hold the college contractually accountable for processing it. Enrolli cannot directly access or modify a college's internal CRM, database, or recruiting system. The college is responsible for removing your student from their own system in accordance with their agreement with Enrolli. If you believe a college has not honored a drop request within ten (10) business days, please contact us at hello@myenrolli.com and we will take action under our College Data Agreement.

You are not required to use Enrolli's drop submission process. You or your student may contact any college directly to request removal from their recruiting pipeline at any time, independent of Enrolli.

5. Your College Connection Page — Opting Out and Dropping

All Enrolli subscribers have access to a College Connection page in their dashboard. This page shows you which colleges have received your information through College Connection, when it was shared, and what triggered the connection.

You can opt out or submit a drop request at any time:

If you want a college to stop receiving your information — or if you want to formally drop from a college's recruiting pipeline — you can do that through the College Connection page:

  • Select the college you want to drop from your College Connection page.
  • Complete a short form. The questions come from that college — they usually want to know where you decided to go, why you opted out, or similar things.
  • Enrolli submits the form to the college on your behalf.
  • The college is required under their agreement with Enrolli to process your request.
  • The college may reach out to you once after receiving your request — this is normal and doesn't mean they're ignoring you. After that one contact, they can't keep reaching out to you based on Enrolli data.

Opting out stops future sharing. Information already sent to a college before your opt-out can't be taken back — that data is in their system. But you can always contact colleges directly too, without going through Enrolli.
